Welcome

I study the formation and orbital evolution of exoplanets, particularly hot Jupiters.

About Me

Hello! I am Yugo Kawai, a researcher focused on the migration history of exoplanets. My work explores the role of disk migration, high-eccentricity migration, and tidal interactions in shaping hot Jupiter populations. I use both observational datasets and hierarchical Bayesian modeling techniques to analyze planetary system architectures.

Research Topics

  • Hot Jupiter Migration: Comparing disk and high-eccentricity migration using obliquity and age diagnostics.
  • Tidal Circularization: Estimating tidal quality factors and identifying dynamically young planets.
  • Inner Companions: Statistical search for small inner planets in hot Jupiter systems.
  • Mass Ratio Distributions: Using hierarchical models to identify features predicted by core accretion.
